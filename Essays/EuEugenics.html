Polygenic Screening Primer

  • a few eggs are extracted from a patient and fertilized
  • each embryo is biopsied for a few cells which are sequenced
  • each embryo is ranked according to a weighted sum of multiple traits
  • the chosen embryo is implanted into the patient

Degree of Controversy

Least Controversial ➟➟

  • ‘decrease suffering’
  • ‘minimal impact’
  • allow people with monogenic disorders to safely have children1

More Controversial ➟➟

  • algorithmically defined traits
  • ‘decreases suffering’ (eg. depression, schizophrenia)
  • possible (significant) impact2

Illegal

  • ‘designer babies’

Ranking

  • ‘likelihood score’ for the selected traits
  • more ‘important’ traits are given a higher weight

How common is it? How expensive?

“Working with 173 Clinics in 37 countries and 6 continents … and growing.”1

  • legal in 30 out of 43 countries in Europe2
  • ~>$3000 ($1000 upfront fee, plus $400 per embryo)3
  • “Over a million babies are born each year via IVF”
  • example: 10% of children in Denmark are born by IVF
  • Denmark and Israel pay for IVF

  2. Survey on ART and IUI: legislation, regulation, funding and registries in European countries: The European IVF-monitoring Consortium (EIM) for the European Society of Human Reproduction and Embryology (ESHRE), Calhaz-Jorge 2020

  3. LifeView, from Genomic Prediction

Does it actually work

  • ‘moderate’ improvements
  • 4 DALYs (Disability Adjusted Life Years)
  • but over generations, the differences accumulate
  • it is a stepping stone to more powerful techniques

The Future

Actively used in Livestock and Pets:

  • IVEP (In Vitro Embryo Production)
    • close to 1 million cows per year
    • most common method of embryo production
  • Germline Editing
    • CRISPR cows, pigs, sheep, goats, rabbits, chickens, and fish
    • widely used to create the breed stocks for IVEP
  • Cloning
    • widely used in livestock, horses, dogs and cats
    • commercialized in several industries

Research Phase

  • IES (Iterated Embryo Selection)
    • Waiting on breakthroughs in cloning– epigenetic difficulties
    • most likely ‘next generation’ in humans
    • huge potential gains across all traits– predicted up to 120 years of life1
  • ‘pygmaliomics’
    • assembly of DNA from selected fragments
    • active research (mammoth deextinction)
  • de-novo DNA synthesis
    • not really significant, will always be expensive, but acts as an upper bound
