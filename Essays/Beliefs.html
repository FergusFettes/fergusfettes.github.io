Gradualism is significant in terms of ethics. I beleive that, to a first approximation, how much mind you have correlates positively with your moral worth. This implies that humans have higher moral value than chickens, and that chickens have higher moral value than grasses. There are lots of cases where the eg. vegan ordering may be disrupted though panpsychist overminds (eg. the web of connections between mushrooms and trees in a forst may be more ‚Äòconscious‚Äô than some simple animals).

Of course, while panpsychism give by making some statements more easy, it also takes away, but making some calculations more difficult. If I want to establish the relative moral ordering of two entities (lets say a chicken and a mushroom, lets say because I want to kill and eat one of them), I can‚Äôt just judge how much of a mind each of them has, but I also need to establish how large are the overminds each is part of, and how important is the entity in question for the functioning of that overmind?

A factory farmed chicken is not really part of any overmind, having been seperated from its ecosystem and its peers. In this way, the factory farmed chicken has less moral worth than one in a forest (where, for example, if I hunt and eat it I am depriving some other members of the ecosystem its mother and its lunch, respectively). On the other hand, if I pick and eat the mushroom I will likely spread the spores it carries quite far‚Äì it is designed to seduce me into taking part in its reproductive cycle after all‚Äì so obviously eating the mushroom causes less harm to conscious beings.

But the conclusion about the chickens‚Äì does the factory farmed chicken have less moral worth? To the extent that its deprivation will rob it of many of its natural affordances, it will indeed have lower moral value. This, of course, is exactly why factory farming is so morally repugnant‚Äì these entities should be part of a more complex overmind, and they have been robbed of that opportunity.

Unless of course you see them as ‚Äògrist for the mill‚Äô‚Äì part of the human techno-overmind, part of the food supplies for the demons of Babylon‚Äì then they get their moral worth back again, but that doesn‚Äôt mean they no longer have to suffer. Ho hum.

The flip side of gradulism is of course that this unhappy fact applies to humans too‚Äì individuals are less important than the overminds, the overminds have more moral worth. We have seen these bloated overminds eat people for millenia.
